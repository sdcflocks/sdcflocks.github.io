IUCG

Islamic University College Ghana


The establishment of the Islamic University College, Ghana started with the acquisition of land in 1998, The first batch of students to be admitted to the college started lectures on 10th April, 2001. That date, therefore marked real beginnings of operations of the college. The college was established entirely with support from the World Ahul Bait Foundation an Iranian Non-Governmental Agency. It is a private non-residential secular institution with accreditation from the National Accreditation Board.


It's mission included producing specialist in areas such as Administration, Banking, Economics, Mangaments, Science and Technology, Secular and Islamic law to provide honest, selfless and dedicated service to the public and private sectors. The Islamic University College, is in the process of being affiliated to the University of Ghana - Legon. By this arrangement, The University of Ghana will be supervising the work of IUCG. The University of Ghana will also be awarding degrees and diploma to IUCG graduates until the university becomes fully autonomous.


The duration of the course is four years (8 semesters). The University College has adopted the semester system of managing the academic year. A semester consists of 16 weeks for teaching, revision, and examinations. Semester dats are announced in University College notices posted on notice boards. The principal methods of instructions are by lectures, tutorials, practical works, assisgnmets course works and project works. Each course of study is normally completed in a semester. Assesments of students is by Continuous Assesments (CA) and Final Examination. The score for the CA shall contribute 30% of the total score for the final (Course Grade), while the score for the final examinations shall contribute 70% of the score for the final grade.
