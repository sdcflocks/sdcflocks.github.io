Central University College


The Central University College is an initiative of the International Gospel Church (ICGC). It has its origins in a short-term pastorial training institute which was started in 1988. The College later expanded its programmes to include an integrated and practice-oriented business and to reflect its new status as a liberal arts university, the name was changed to Central University College in 1999.


The College now has four Schools, namely, Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences, the School of Theology and Missions, School of Applied Sciences and the School of Business Management and Administration and it is affiliated to the University of Cape Coast. In 2004/2005, total student population was about 2000. Today, student population is far in excess of this figure. The College plans to offer courses in areas of Science, medicine, engineering and other related disciplines in the near future. The only new school, School of Arts and Social Sciences admitted students for the 2009/2010 academic year.


THE DISTINCTIVE FEATURES OF THE CENTRAL UNIVERSITY COLLEGE ARE:


INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY USAGE AND PRACTICE

Information technology usage and practice have become intergrated into all the programmes of the University and students are expected to be conversant with basic computer technology and the various software packages in their areas of specializaton.


FRENCH LANGUEAGE PROFICIENCY

To enable students to have a competitive edge on the job market, French language studies is compulsory for all students in the Business School and also for those in the B.A Theology programmes.


WORKER-FRIENDLY PROGRAMMES

The proogrammes have been structured to allow full time workers to participate fully. The morning session starts from 8:00am and ends at 2:00pm. The afternoon session starts at 4:30pm and ends at 8:30pm. The Week-end programme runs from evening, 5:00pm to 8:50pm and Saturday 8:00am.
