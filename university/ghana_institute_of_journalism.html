GIJ

Ghana Institute of Journalism


The Ghana Institute of Journalism, formerly Ghana School of Journalism was officially opened on the 16th of October 1959, to provide training in journalism towards the development of a patriotic cadre of journalist to play an active role in the emancipation of the African continent. It was initially a department of the Accra Technical Institute (now Accra Polytechnic).

After the 1966 coup, the school moved to the present site which used to house the Ghana Press Club. In 1974 the National Redemption Council (NRC) passed its first legislative instrument NRCD 275 formally establishing the Ghana Institute of Journalism. Its vision is to become a leading institute for communication studies in Africa and the mission is to turn out professionals with requisite skill, knowledge and attitudes to meet the expectations of modern society. The objects of the institute are to train students to become truly African in their professional outlook, broaden their intellectual scope and equip them with modern trend in communication techniques and development communication.

Studying at ghana_institute_of_journalism offers numerous advantages. The school attracts lecturers and speakers who are experts in the field as well as students who already work in media and communication. Thus students gain invaluable opportunities to learn from peers and practitioners.

The Institute is conveniently located at Ringway, Osu, close to the centre of major businesses and Ministries of Government in Accra. It is a few minutes from the Parliament House, Accra Conference Centre, National Theatre, and main sports stadium, making it easy for students to cover a wide range of events.


The Institute is administered by a governing council, an executive management board, the academic board, and other departments, units and committees.
